About the Project

A Homecoming Through the Lens

Himeji Castle, affectionately known as "White Heron Castle," is a World Heritage site located in my hometown. Depending on the angle, season, and time of day, it reveals completely different expressions.

Just as Katsushika Hokusai depicted the multifaceted nature of Mt. Fuji in his "Thirty-Six Views," I have decided to record 36 "present moments" of this castle using photography as my modern brush. This is not merely a record, but a journey of dialogue with my hometown.
